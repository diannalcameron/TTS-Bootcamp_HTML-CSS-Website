I'm a new mom, and I really like riding bikes.

Momma Bear Rides Bikes is an idea I have for a nonprofit cycling clothing line that promotes cycling safety and the humanization of cyclists.

What if the first thought drivers had when they saw a cyclist was, "That person is a mom," or, "That person is important to somebody?" Would it make them slow down? Would it make them drive more cautiously? Would they take the extra ten seconds to share the road?

I think when we see cyclists as just "cyclists," rather than people -- individuals -- it can fuel that road rage, but if drivers see cyclists as human-beings first, maybe lives could be saved.
